The Weird Part of my life

So the weird part of my life is what makes me weird and wonderful. I have a rare(not so rare)condition called Hypermobile Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome. It makes me incredibly flexible in all my joints, kind of like Elastigirl in the film The Incredibles. Its my version of a super power even when its not so super at times and more difficult to live with. Ill give you a quick rundown of what Hypermobile Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome (which is also known as hEDS) is and then how it affects me.

What is Hypermobile

The Ehlers-Danlos syndromes (EDS) are a group of thirteen individual genetic conditions, all of which affect the body’s connective tissue. Connective tissue lies between other tissues and organs, keeping these separate whilst connecting them, holding everything in place and providing support, like the mortar between bricks. In EDS, a gene mutation causes a certain kind of connective tissue – the kind will depend on the type of EDS but usually a form of collagen – to be fragile and stretchy. This stretchiness can sometimes be seen in the skin of someone with EDS; individuals with the condition may also be able to extend their joints further than is usual – this is known as being hypermobile, bendy or double-jointed. As collagen is present throughout the body, people with EDS tend to experience a broad range of symptoms, most of them less visible than the skin and joint differences. These are complex syndromes affecting many systems of the body at once, despite this EDS is often an invisible disability. Symptoms commonly include, but are not limited to, long-term pain, chronic fatigue, dizziness, palpitations and digestive disorders. Such problems and their severity vary considerably from person to person, even in the same type of EDS and within the same family.

My life living with hEDS

So for me living my youth life with what I was told was "just double jointed" was great I was bendy more so than most of my friends even the ones who went to acrobatics/gymnastics. I did have very weak ankles growing up and often sprained my ankles just by standing on a small stone was enough for me to go over/sprain them. I remember my brother who was about 4 or 5 years old was playing with his golf set and I had a swollen ankle from spraining it at school while wearing white patterned socks which made my ankle looked like a golf ball my brother accidently hit my ankle with his golf club. Im still not so sure it was an accident. The fun things I taught myself how to do split, flips and more before I went to acrobatics when I was about 11 years old. I had no idea how weird my life was about to become. I went to college at the age of 17 and thats where I suffered my first dislocation.

My First dislocation

I was 2 weeks off being 18 years old when I stood up from sitting on my bed in my dorm room and I crumbled to the floor. I had no idea at the time but I had Dislocated and relocated my right patella. I tried to rest for a few days in my dorm room, not moving much apart from going to the toliet which I had an en-suite dorm room. After resting a few days I went back to class and I thought all went. It did go well until I went to my class mates dorm room. I literally took 2 steps into my friends dorm and down I went. The pain I won't forget. I had fully dislocated my right patella again but this time I landed on it badly. It was that bad i knew i had to go home from college. I arrived home and couldn't stand so my mum placed my matteress on the floor
